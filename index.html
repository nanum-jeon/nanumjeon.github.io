Nanum Jeon

Nanum Jeon, a Ph.D. Candidate in Sociology and an M.S. Student in Statistics and Data Science at UCLA, is forging a unique path at the intersection of sociology, statistics, and data science. She is an active member of the Social Inequality Data Science Lab (SIDS-Lab) and the Lab for Statistics, Computing, Algorithms, Learning, and Economics (SCALE).


Her research primarily focuses on social demography, social stratification, and inequality, and involves applying statistical and machine learning methods in sociological research. The focus of her academic endeavors is to analyze the effects of life shocks, such as divorce and job displacement, using longitudinal data for causal inference. This includes examining their impacts on individual life trajectories and the processes of social stratification, particularly in relation to labor market participation and family dynamics.

Nanum Jeon

Nanum Jeon, a Ph.D. Candidate of Sociology and an M.S. Student of Statistics and Data Science at UCLA, is forging a unique path at the intersection of sociology and statistics and data science. She is an active member of the Social Inequality Data Science Lab (SIDS-Lab) and the Lab for Statistics, Computing, Algorithms, Learning, and Economics (SCALE). Her research is deeply rooted in social demography, social stratification, and inequality, and the use of statistical methods in social sciences. In her dissertation, Nanum explores the effects of disruptive life events on life outcomes, with a specific focus on labor market engagement and family dynamics. Furthermore, she is keenly interested in how these complex questions can be studied and understood through statistical analysis. Nanum is also engaged in exploring the integration of machine learning with social science research, showcasing a unique blend of sociology and data science.
